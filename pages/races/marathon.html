Humpy's Marathon

Sunday, August 22, 2021 -- 8:30 AM


Course Overview

The 26.2 mile course begins in the heart of downtown Anchorage. Runners head west on 6th Avenue, north on H Street, then straight ahead on Christensen Drive to 2nd Avenue where the course turns left to join the Tony Knowles Coastal Trail. They run past Westchester Lagoon, Earthquake Park, Postmark Drive and Point Woronzof. Runners turn around about 100 yards beyond a point known by locals as “the Blue Bridge.” They run back north to Westchester Lagoon where they veer right (east) on the Chester Creek Trail. From the Chester Creek turnaround (the roundabout at the base of the hill beyond, or east of, the bridge to East High School) runners return to the lagoon and head back to the finish line in downtown Anchorage.

The marathon course is USATF certified and it is a Boston Marathon qualifier.
In 2016, 17% of the marathon finishers ran a Boston Marathon qualifying time.

Field Limit

The marathon field is limited to the first 500 entrants.

Start/Finish

The marathon starts and finishes on 6th Avenue between F and G Street adjacent to the Performing Arts Center in downtown Anchorage.



Time Cut-off

The course closes at 3:30 PM. Runners must cross the finish line by 3:30 PM to be considered official finishers. Runners must reach Valley of the Moon Park aid station at mile 23 by 2:30 PM.

Participants who register for the marathon but need more time to finish than the allotted 7 hours, may start early with the walkers. However, if they finish in less than 7 hours, after starting with the walkers, they will not be ranked nor be eligible for awards.


Registration Goodies

  • Long-sleeve technical shirt (see 2019 shirt below)
  • Finisher's medal (see 2019 medal below)
  • Professional timing
  • Food and beverage at the finish line



Awards

RunFest awards are given to the top three over-all male and female finishers as well as the top three male and female finishers in each 5 year age group. The top three finishers will receive awards in their respective age groups as well. Awards will be distributed in Town Square Park. As in the past, the first place male and female marathon finishers will each win a round trip certificate good for coach travel on Alaska Airlines.

All awards are based on chip times -- the time elapsed from when a runner crosses the timing mat at start line till when that runner crosses the timing mat at the finish line.


Sleeping Lady Challenge

The Anchorage Runfest is one of four Anchorage Running Club yearly events. Successful completion of eligible races in three of these four events can qualify a runner for the unique Sleeping Lady Challenge medal. See more information regarding the Sleeping Lady Challenge.
