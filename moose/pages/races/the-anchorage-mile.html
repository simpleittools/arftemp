The Anchorage Mile

Saturday, August 20, 2016 -- 11:00 AM


Course Overview

The Anchorage Mile begins in downtown Anchorage on the south side of the Delaney Park Strip at the intersection of West 10th Avenue and G Street. Runners will complete one and a half loops of the following course:

  • Head east (towards the mountains) until reaching E Street. Turn left on E Street (north) and left again (west) on West 9th Street. Continue along the edge of the Delaney Park Strip till reaching I Street. Take another left turn (south). Turn left (east) again at West 10th Avenue. Cross the starting line. This is one loop.

The second half loop ends at the intersection of G Street and West 9th Avenue -- on the north side of the Delaney Park Strip.

The Anchorage Mile is USATF certified.

Field Limit

The Anchorage Mile field is limited to the first 150 entrants. In 2015, 115 individuals ran The Anchorage Mile.


Start/Finish

The Anchorage Mile will start on 10th Avenue between H Street and I Streets. The finish will be at the intersection of 9th Avenue and G Street. See the schedule below for heat start times.


Heats

The race will consist of six heats.

  • #1 - 11:00 AM:  females running over a 6 minute / mile pace
  • #2 - 11:15 AM:  males running over a 6 minute / mile pace
  • #3 - 11:30 AM:  fast females running under a 6 minute / mile pace
  • #4 - 11:45 AM:  fast males running under a 6 minute / mile pace
  • #5 - 12:00 PM:  The Military Mile*
  • #6 - 12:15 PM:  males and females (combined) running over a 12 minute / mile pace 

*Information about our new Military Mile will be forthcoming.


Awards / Prizes

ARF will be offering cash prizes for The Anchorage Mile. In 2015 awards for runners in the fastest male and female heats were:

  • 1st Place: $75
  • 2nd Place: $50
  • 3rd Place: 25

Additionally, the first male and/or female to break the previous record for The Anchorage Mile will receive a bonus $50. All prize money earned by NCAA athletes will be allocated in accordance with NCAA regulations.

We are currently researching data to see if we can determine an Alaska record for the male and female outdoor mile. If we can determine an existing record we will be offering yet another bonus for anyone breaking it. Stay tuned as this project continues and results are announced.

All finishers will receive an event medal.
