United Physical Therapy 49K Ultra

Sunday, August 21, 2016 -- 7:00 AM


Course Overview

Our 49K Ultra is meant for those interested in a bit more of a challenge than that presented by the marathon distance. And then there’s the uniqueness of running a 49K in the 49th state in the Union.

The 49K Ultra (or 30.44860 miles for the metrically challenged) course begins in the heart of downtown Anchorage. Runners head west on 6th Avenue, north on H Street, then straight ahead on Christensen Drive to 2nd Avenue where the course turns left to join the Tony Knowles Coastal Trail. They run past Westchester Lagoon, Earthquake Park, Postmark Drive and Point Woronzof. Their first turnaround point is approximately 1/4 mile below the top of the Kincaid hill by the chalet. Back at Westchester Lagoon runners bear right (east) on the Chester Creek Trail. From the Chester Creek turnaround (a bit beyond the branch-off bridge to East High School) runners return to the lagoon and head back to the finish line in downtown Anchorage.

There are five uphills of significance:

1. Earthquake Park at mile 4
2. Point Woronzof at about mile 5.5
3. Kincaid Park at mile 10.5
4. Near Goose Lake at about mile 23.5
5. Christensen hill at about mile 29.5.

 

Download the course map (pdf file)


Field Limit

The 49K field is limited to the first 150 entrants. 90 runners completed the 49K in 2015.

Start/Finish

The ultramarathon starts and finishes on 6th Avenue between F and G Street adjacent to the Performing Arts Center in downtown Anchorage. Start time is 7:00 AM.


Time Cut-off

The course closes at 2:30 PM. Runners must cross the finish line at 2:30 PM to be considered official finishers. Runners must reach Valley of the Moon Park aid station at mile 27 by 1:30 PM. Transportation from Valley of the Moon Park back to the start/finish area will be provided.


Registration Goodies

  • Long-sleeve technical shirt
  • Finisher's medal
  • Professional timing
  • Food and beverage at the finish line

49K Ultra Awards

Awards will given to the top male and female finishers and to the first runner to the turnaround on Kincaid hill. In 2015 the first place male and female 49K Ultra finishers each won a round trip travel certificate on Alaska Airlines. 2016 awards have yet to be confirmed. All awards are based on gun times -- the time elapsed from the sound of the start gun to when a runner crosses the timing mat at the finish line.
