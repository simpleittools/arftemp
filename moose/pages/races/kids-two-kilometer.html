Children's Hospital at Providence
Kids' 2K

Saturday, August 20, 2016 -- 10:00 AM


About the Kids' 2K

Thanks to the sponsorship of the Children's Hospital at Providence (CHAP) and the generous support of Healthy Futures Alaska, the CHAP Kids' 2K is free to any child who wants to participate! In 2015, more than 750 kids registered for this event.

One of the goals of ARF and Healthy Futures Alaska is to create more opportunities for kids to be active through play and exercise and to teach them healthy life skills that will stick with them into adulthood. 2016 will be the fifth year the Kids' 2K is held on its own day. Parents have requested a later starting time and we have listened. This year's event begins a half hour later than last year. Participants have more room to spread out and more time to learn about fun ways to be active.


Course, Start/Finish

CHAP Kids' 2K (about 1.2 miles) is a no-pressure fun run held on and immediately adjacent to the Delaney Parkstrip's uninterrupted grassy block between E and I Streets (one block south of the Dena'ina Civic and Convention Center). The start area is on 9th Avenue at its intersection with G Street. The finish is on the Park Strip grass (near F Street). The pre-race warm-up led by Anchorage's Healthy Heroes (local elite athletes) starts at 9:30 AM and the fun run begins at 10:00 AM. Our Healthy Heroes guide the runners around the course.

Registration

Although, the event is free, all participants must register and receive their bibs before the start of the race. Parents can register their children online or in person at the Health and Fitness Expo. Donations are welcome to help defray the costs of putting on this event. Parents are welcome to run with their kids but need not register. Children must be 12 years of age or younger to participate.


Registration Goodies

  • Running hat
  • Finisher's medal (received at the finish line on Saturday)
  • Food and fun at the finish line

Family Health and Safety Day

Come be a part of Anchorage RunFest's "Family Health and Safety Day". The event will take place immediately following the Kids' 2K. If you are an interested vendor, please contact Michaela Orzechowski.

Celebrate wellness at our event with varied health and safety education for kids and families. Fitness activities, face painting, obstacle courses and demonstrations - plus family-friendly activities featuring the "Healthy Heros" from Healthy Futures.


After the Race

After the Kids' 2K, stick around to watch The Anchorage Mile -- a sprint race circling the park strip. See the course here. After that you might want to walk over to the Dena'ina Center to check out the Health and Fitness Expo.
